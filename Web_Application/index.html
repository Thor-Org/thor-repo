Transforming the backbone of Cyber Security

Discover the role of pseudo random numbers in cyber security and how our project is spearheading the path to less predictable, more random data.

the Problem

The generation of random numbers is too important to be left to chance.

- Robert Coveyou

Random numbers are the backbone of security and encryption algorithms. Therefore, if an attacker can predict the random numbers used to generate your encryption keys, then they can overcome your security.   

Modern best practices rely on pseudo random number generators (PRNGs) that are based on mathematical and deterministic algorithms, to create random numbers. However, these PRNGs lack the level of entropy needed to ensure optimal security. Consequently, as computer processing  power increases so too does hacker's ability to predict these numbers, placing your data at an ever increasing danger.

If we ever hope to improve our encryption, we need to move away from these vulnerable PRGNs and explore other techniques for creating random numbers.

our Solution

Lightning never strikes the same place twice.

- Proverb

Computers are a notoriously bad source for randomness as they are deterministic by default. Therefore, our team decided to use natural phenomena as a source of randomness because as it turns out, the real world is "unpredictable."

We specifically choose to use data gathered from the measurement of lightning strikes for this project. Lightning is a great source for us because it consistently produces chaotic data, in high volume, that never identically repeats.
